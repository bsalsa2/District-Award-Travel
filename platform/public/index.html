── FAQ

Fair questions, straight answers.

Honestly — how do I know this isn't a scam?
Fair question — here's how this works. We never ask for account passwords. You type or screenshot your balances yourself. You book everything yourself, in your own accounts. The only money that ever moves is our invoice — sent after your savings are documented with a timestamped cash benchmark. If we save you nothing, you pay nothing.
How do you calculate 'savings'?
We capture the real cash price of your trip (timestamped screenshot of Google Flights or the airline's site) at the moment we find your award option. Savings = that cash price minus everything you actually pay out of pocket (award taxes and fees). Our fee is 10% of that documented number — you see the receipt.
What if I don't like the options?
You owe nothing. No savings, no fee — and that includes 'we looked and found nothing good for your dates.' We'll tell you that honestly instead of forcing a mediocre redemption.
How long does this take?
Options typically land in your inbox within days. If your route or dates are genuinely hard, we'll tell you up front instead of leaving you waiting.
Which programs do you work with?
Chase Ultimate Rewards, Amex Membership Rewards, Capital One, Citi, Bilt, the major U.S. airlines and hotel programs, and the international transfer partners where the real value hides — 15+ programs in total.
Is my data safe?
We store your name, contact info, and the loyalty balances you choose to share — never account passwords, never card numbers. We never sell data, and you can ask us to delete everything at any time. Details in our Privacy Policy.
Why can't I just Google this?
Google Flights doesn't show award space, transfer partners, or sweet spots. The seat that costs $4,300 in cash and 75,000 points exists — it just doesn't show up where most people look. Knowing where to look is the entire service.
Who's behind this?
District Award Travel is a student-founded advisory based in the DC area. We're small on purpose, we put our live savings ledger at the top of this page, and every savings number we claim is documented with a timestamped benchmark.
